Space activity

For centuries, people dreamed about leaving Earth and travelling to other worlds. Then, in 1957, the Soviet Union made the first small step into space by launching a small satellite, called Sputnik. Early space activities were dominated by the United States and the Soviet Union, today, countries such as China, India, South Korea, Israel and Brazil are developing their own space industries.

During the last 60 years, probes have been sent towards all of the planets in the Solar System, as well as many satellites, asteroids and comets. Spacecraft have landed on half a dozen worlds, while rovers have driven over the surfaces of the Moon and Mars.

Since Yuri Gagarin paved the way in 1961, more than 500 people have flown in space. 20 people have travelled to the Moon and 12 of them have walked on the Moon. Space stations have been built, and astronauts have learned to live and work in space for many months, or even years.
